How it works

Five primitives. No hardcoded rules.

Otto exposes five low-level actions to the model. Every command you give resolves to one of them. There's no rigid command list — Otto retrieves the most relevant capability templates and hands them to the model as recipes to fill in.

📜
run_applescript

Runs AppleScript in-process to drive any scriptable Mac app.

⌨️
press_key

Sends real keystrokes via CGEvent — shortcuts, combos, repeats.

👁️
read_screen

Reads the accessibility tree of the focused app to see what's there.

🌐
open_url

Opens a URL in your browser of choice.

🎥
obs_call

Drives OBS Studio over its WebSocket for streaming & recording.

…and a growing library of capability templates built on top of them.

01

Summon

Hit ⌥ Space. Type a command, or hold the mic and talk.

02

Retrieve

Otto finds the most relevant capabilities for what you said and gives them to the model.

03

Act

The model fills in a primitive and Otto runs it — keystrokes, scripts, the works.

04

Reflect

Afterwards it reviews the session and updates its memory for next time.

How it learns

It rewrites its own memory.

You never train Otto explicitly. After each session it runs a lightweight retrospective, looks at what you said and what actually worked, and folds your phrasing into its capability library — local to your machine.

During
Every turn is logged as a structured event — what you asked, which primitive ran, what happened.
After
A cheap gpt-4o-mini pass (or a local Ollama model) reviews the session and extracts reusable patterns.
Learned
Your phrasing is added to an existing capability or becomes a new one in capabilities.user.json.
Next time
The way you say something lands instantly. Browse, edit, or undo anything from the journal — ⌥ ⇧ Space.
💡 It compounds. Use Otto normally and it quietly gets better at fitting the exact way you work — no setup, no prompt engineering.

Extend it

Add your own capabilities.

Capabilities are plain JSON. Drop a new one in, restart, and Otto can do it — with the same retrieve-and-fill flow as everything else.

// memory/capabilities.json
{
  "id": "spotify-play",
  "description": "Play music in Spotify",
  "examples": ["play Spotify", "start music", "resume playback"],
  "primitive": "run_applescript",
  "template": "tell application \"Spotify\" to play",
  "required_apps": ["Spotify"]
}

required_apps keeps a capability out of the index unless one of the listed apps is actually installed.
